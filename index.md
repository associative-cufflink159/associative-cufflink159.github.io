## 🛠️ How it Works

The engine uses a local-first approach. This means the software runs entirely on your machine. No information leaves your computer unless you explicitly choose to share it.

The system uses an async multi-agent brain. This phrase means the program runs several background tasks at once to handle different parts of your story. One agent manages the plot, another tracks character traits, and a third handles world details. These agents talk to each other to maintain a consistent narrative. 

All of your data sits in plain files on your disk. You can view these files with any basic text editor. This structure ensures you always own your work. You can move, edit, or back up your story files easily.

## 💡 Using the Software

Upon opening openovel, you see a blank workspace. You start a new project by clicking the New Story button. 

The main dashboard shows your character list, your world settings, and your current story flow. You type your prompts in the main text box. The agents process your input and suggest paths for the narrative. You can accept these suggestions or write your own direction.

The software tracks your story state. If you close the program, your progress saves automatically. You can resume your work at any time by opening the project file from the dashboard menu.

## 🛡️ Privacy and Safety

This software does not use a cloud server. Your credentials, story data, and agent configurations remain trapped inside your local folder. This design choice prevents data leaks and keeps your creative property safe from outside interference. 

Because the program is self-hosted, you are responsible for your own backups. Copy your story folders to an external drive or a private cloud folder to protect against file loss.

## 🏗️ Managing Your Narrative Engine

The agents in openovel use a format called a narrative engine. This engine reads your story files like a map. If you want to change the way an agent behaves, you can edit the configuration file located within your project folder. 

1. Open your project folder.
2. Locate the file labeled `config.json`.
3. Open the file in Notepad or another simple text tool.
4. Modify the settings according to your preference.
5. Save the file and restart the application.

This level of detail gives you full control over the tone and style of your artificial agents. You can create a dark mystery, a cheerful fantasy, or a technical guide. The agents adapt to the rules you build.
